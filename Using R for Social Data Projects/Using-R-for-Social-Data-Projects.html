Participants use them as an opportunity to practice their programming skills, work on algorithmic efficiency, or challenge themselves to learn something new. Often, participants share the code for their submissions, helping others learn how to apply new techniques to their work.

The prompts are time-bound or time-specific but flexible in their execution. For example, the 30 Day Map Challenge challenges participants to create something new every day in November.

Although these projects are often language-agnostic, R users often take part. For example, genuary invites programmers to create generative art based on a new prompt every day in January.
